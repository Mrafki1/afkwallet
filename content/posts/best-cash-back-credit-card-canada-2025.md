Not everyone wants to deal with points programs and transfer partners. Cash back cards give you real dollars back with no redemption complexity. Here are the best ones in Canada right now, broken down by use case.

## Best overall cash back card: Scotia Momentum Visa Infinite

**Earn rates:** 4% on grocery & recurring bills, 2% on gas & transit, 1% everywhere else
**Annual fee:** First year free (then $120)
**Best portal:** GCR or Frugal Flyer ($100 back)

4% on groceries and subscriptions is the best rate you'll find on a premium cash back card. A household spending $1,000/month on groceries earns $480/year in that category alone — nearly covering the $120 annual fee before other spending.

The FYF offer makes year one a no-brainer. Cash back is paid out once a year in November.

**Best for:** Households with high grocery and recurring bill spend.

## Best flat-rate cash back: Brim World Elite Mastercard

**Earn rate:** 2% on all purchases (up to $25,000/yr)
**Annual fee:** First year free (then $199)

Brim gives you 2% on everything with no categories to track, plus **no foreign transaction fees** — a rare combination. Points are worth 1¢ each and redeem as statement credits.

The lounge access (Priority Pass) and travel insurance package round out a strong premium card. Best for people who want simplicity and travel frequently.

**Best for:** High spenders who want a simple flat rate and travel internationally.

## Best no-fee cash back: BMO CashBack Mastercard

**Earn rates:** 3% on grocery, 1% on recurring bills
**Annual fee:** $0

3% on groceries with no annual fee is the best free grocery rate in Canada. No income requirement makes it accessible.

**Best for:** Anyone who wants strong grocery rewards without paying a fee.

## Best cash back for dining: Simplii Financial Cash Back Visa

**Earn rate:** 4% on restaurants & bars
**Annual fee:** $0

No other no-fee card in Canada earns 4% on restaurants. If you spend $500/month eating out, that's $240/year back for free.

**Best for:** People who eat out frequently and don't want to pay an annual fee.

## Best cash back for gas: CIBC Dividend Visa Infinite

**Earn rates:** 4% on gas & grocery, 2% on dining & transit
**Annual fee:** First year free (then $120)
**Best portal:** CCG ($150 back)

CIBC's Dividend card is underrated. 4% on gas AND grocery is the best dual-category rate available. The CCG portal cash back is also among the highest for any CIBC card.

**Best for:** Drivers who also want strong grocery rewards.

## Best cash back for US/international spending: Rogers World Elite Mastercard

**Earn rates:** 3% on USD purchases, 2% on all other purchases
**Annual fee:** $0 (requires $15,000 annual spend OR $29.99/mo)
**Best portal:** GCR or Frugal Flyer ($25 back)

The Rogers World Elite is the only no-fee card in Canada that earns cash back on USD purchases above the FX fee rate. If you shop online at US retailers or travel to the US regularly, 3% on USD spending offsets most of the 2.5% FX fee and leaves you ahead.

**Best for:** Rogers/Fido/Shaw customers and frequent US shoppers.

## Best cash back for Amazon: MBNA Amazon.ca Rewards Mastercard

**Earn rate:** 2.5% at Amazon.ca and Whole Foods (Prime members)
**Annual fee:** $0

If you're an Amazon Prime member with significant Amazon spending, this is the best card for that purpose. Points redeem directly at Amazon checkout with no friction.

**Best for:** Heavy Amazon shoppers with Prime membership.

## How to stack cash back cards

Most churners run a combination:
- **Category card** for groceries/gas (Scotia Momentum or CIBC Dividend)
- **Dining card** for restaurants (Simplii)
- **Flat-rate or USD card** for everything else (Brim or Rogers)

This approach maximizes cash back across all spending without the complexity of a points program.
